The Aztec ball game, known as ullamaliztli, was an ancient Mesoamerican game that may have originated in the ancient Olmec civilization. When the Aztecs settled in a new land, they built two important things to them. The first was a shrine to the god Huitzilopochtli, and the second was a ball court next to the shrine.


In Mesoamerican mythology the game was essential to the story of two Maya gods Hun Hunahpu and Vucub Hunahpu. The gods were tricked into descending to the underworld, where they were challenged to a ball game. The loser, Hun Hunahpus, had his head cut off, which became a common practice among ancient civilizations.

The
Players

The players were men who were mainly warriors or war captives who were often forced to play the game. However, players could be professionals or amateurs. The players wore protective gear such as belts and padding around sensible areas.

RElIGION

The Aztec ball game had a lot of ritual significance. It was mean to mirror the ball court of the heavens, this being the ball court of the underworld where the sun passed each night. The game represented the battle between day and night, and so was also related to the human blood sacrifices that were intended to keep the sun moving in the sky.

Rules

The rules are of the ball game varied across the different empires and periods. However, there were some common guidelines present in all the variations.


1. The players were not allowed to touch the ball with their hands.

2. Teams of 2 to 7 people had to face each other in a court shaped like a capital “I”.

3. The game was supposed to be played with a solid rubber ball.

4. The objective was to get the ball through a stone hoop.

5.Players were able to get points by hitting or interacting markers or lines painted in the court.

The Aztec ball court was known as the tlachtli or tlahco. The courts were usually made up of a narrow central aisle with enclosed endzones at the ends giving the shape of I. The courts were flat surfaces and were surrounded by walls. The ball courts varied by size throughout the empire Aztec but were between 100 and 200 feet long. Some settlements had more than one court. The courts were often placed by the ceremonial centers, temples, and funerary shrines.
The
Outcome

A win or a loss was very much taken to heart. At times, the game would turn into an excuse for an assassination or attack. The game might also be used to settle differences between two teams.


Winners received trophies which included hachas and palmas. These were often associated with the ceremonial items in the Aztec culture.


Since games often had a religious meaning, the losing captain or sometimes the entire team, were sacrificed to the gods.
